Our Mission

About Liberty

A healthy ecosystem provides for all its residents. The current economic system in South LA is overrun by outside developers who extract wealth from the community, denying us the right to self-determination and community building. Liberty Ecosystem seeks to liberate the Crenshaw community with non-extractive financing and social impact investing, creating a self-sustaining, democratically governed economy.

Our Family of Organizations

Liberty Community Land Trust

Liberty Community Land Trust (LCLT) is the only Black-led, Black focused community land trust operating in California. As housing prices in surrounding neighborhoods skyrocket, LCLT is focused on acquiring housing in the areas surrounding the mall to preserve affordable housing, eventually transitioning tenants into owners of housing cooperatives.

Learn More

Liberty Community Empowerment

In any community-centered economic ecosystem, a local source of financing and capital is vital to ensure that responsible community development is prioritized. Liberty Community Empowerment is a source for non-extractive financing that builds community wealth by increasing home ownership and entrepreneurship.

Learn More

Umoja Center

Named after the Swahili word meaning "unity," the Umoja Center is a home for connecting our Crenshaw community specifically and the local Black community generally to the global struggle for justice.

Learn More

Crenshaw Subway Coalition

Established in 2011 as an independent organization, the Crenshaw Subway Coalition seeks to advance community involvement in the Crenshaw-LAX Light Rail Project and Crenshaw Boulevard development process.

Learn More

Downtown Crenshaw Rising

Downtown Crenshaw is a legacy-defining collaborative project to purchase and develop the Baldwin Hills Crenshaw Mall into a net positive urban village with just over 40 acres of quality retail, restaurants, and grocery options, beautiful housing affordable, professional office space to encourage collaboration among our business enterprises, a hotel, entertainment space, and green space.This would all be grounded in the principles of community-wealth building, matched with a neighborhood stabilization fund and featuring community ownership at every stage.
